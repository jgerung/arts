Introduction
BY EVAN HENERSON
LA.COM

“It Sucks to Be Me,” the characters (puppets, really) complain in a song from the Tony-Winning best musical “Avenue Q.”

Part homage to “Sesame Street” (It’s Not Easy Being Green,” get it?), the cheeky and definitely adult-themed musical opens at the Ahmanson on Sept. 7. It’s just one of the many fun shows and performers heading for So Cal this autumn.

(By the way, the “Avenue Q” characters live in a gray outer borough of New York City; so, of course, it sucks being them.)

On the other hand, we in the sunny Southland where it clearly doesn’t suck get to say hello to Salvadore Dali this fall, with a exhibition of the surrealist’s film-related works at the Los Angeles County Museum of Art; see the L.A. Opera in a rare performance of Beethoven’s only opera, “Fidelio,” and enjoy countless great musical acts — from classical stars Itzhak Perlman, Anne-Sophie Mutter, Lang Lang and Andr·s Schiff at Walt Disney Concert Hall to pop acts like Beyonce, Stevie Wonder, Lucinda Williams, Justin Timberlake, Dave Matthews and the Eagles, who are teaming up with the Dixie Chicks to become the opening concert at the Nokia Theater in downtown L.A. on Oct. 18.

The 7,100-seat theater is the centerpiece of L.A. Live, a 27-acre entertainment, residential and commercial complex opening later this year across from Staples Center. The complex includes a smaller venue — Club Nokia, which will accommodate about 2,400.

On Thursday, the American Music Awards announced it would hold its 35th anniversary show at the Nokia Theater on Nov. 18, with an ABC live broadcast marking the national TV debut of the venue.

What follows is really only the tip of the iceberg. We offer highlights and listings, but continue to go online at LA.COM for updates and more events.
